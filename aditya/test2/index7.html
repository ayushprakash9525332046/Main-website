Disaster-Shield

In Case of Drought

1. Conserve Water: Limit water use for non-essential activities like washing cars, watering lawns, or filling swimming pools.

2. Use Water Efficiently: Fix leaks, use water-saving devices, and only run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.

3. Harvest Rainwater: Collect and store rainwater for watering plants and other uses.

4. Store Drinking Water: Ensure you have enough clean drinking water stored for emergencies.

5. Plant Drought-resistant Crops: If you are a farmer, switch to drought-resistant crops that require less water.

6. Reduce Water Pollution: Avoid polluting water sources, as water will be scarcer and cleaner water is more crucial during a drought.

7. Stay Informed: Monitor water restrictions and follow local guidelines to conserve water.
