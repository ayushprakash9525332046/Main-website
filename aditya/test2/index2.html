Disaster-Shield

In Case Of Heavy Rainfall


1-Monitor Weather Alerts: Stay updated with weather reports and warnings from trusted sources like local authorities, weather apps, or news channels.

2-Avoid Flood-prone Areas: Stay away from low-lying areas, riverbanks, and flood-prone regions during heavy rains.

3-Secure Your Home: Ensure that your house is structurally sound, fix leaks, and secure loose items in your yard or balcony that could be swept away.

4-Prepare an Emergency Kit: Have an emergency kit ready with essentials like water, food, a first aid kit, flashlights, batteries, and important documents.

5-Drainage Maintenance: Clean drains, gutters, and channels around your home to prevent water from accumulating and causing floods.

6-Turn Off Electrical Appliances: Unplug appliances and turn off electricity in case of severe flooding to avoid electrocution or short circuits.

7-Safe Drinking Water: Store drinking water, as floods can contaminate water supplies. Use boiled or bottled water for drinking and cooking.
