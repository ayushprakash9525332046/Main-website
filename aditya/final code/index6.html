Disaster-Shield

In Case of Landslide


1. Be Aware of Warning Signs: Cracks in the ground, unusual water flow, and tilting trees can indicate a potential landslide.

2. Evacuate Immediately: If local authorities issue a landslide warning, leave the area immediately and move to higher ground.

3. Stay Away from Landslide Areas: After a landslide, avoid the area to prevent further risks.

4. Protect Yourself: If you cannot evacuate, move to an upper floor of your building and protect your head.

5. Have an Emergency Plan: Create an evacuation plan for your family and know the safe routes.

6. Avoid Driving: Do not drive during a landslide, as roads may be blocked or unstable.

7. Stay Updated: Follow local news and weather reports for updates on landslide risks.
